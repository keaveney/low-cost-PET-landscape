SCINTILLATION MATERIAL

TYPE

A good scintillator is one with a high atomic number, high density, high light yield and fast decay time 1. These qualities of the scintillation material affects the sensitivity, scatter fraction, temporal resolution, energy resolution and temporal resolution of the detector.

BGO

  • Previously most commonly used in PET for its high stopping power 1 2 3
  • Replaced due to its slower decay time and low light output (9000 photons / MeV) 1 3
  • TOF not possible 1
  • BGO is a third of the price of LYSO 2

LYSO

  • New industry standard that has replaced BGO 1 2
  • Has a high stopping power, good light output (30000 photons / MeV) and a significantly faster decay time than BGO 1 2
  • High light output increases energy resolution 3
  • Energy resolution (10-14%) and a timing resolution of (450-600ps)
  • Biggest downside is it high cost 1

LFS

  • Being investigated as a replacement for LYSO 1
  • Timing resolution of 100ps and energy resolution of 10% 1
  • More research needs to be done 1
